#  Actors and Activities 



 <h2 align="center">Types of Economic Activities</h2>

There are many ways to characterise the economic activities into sectors. One of the common ways is to use the three or five sector classification of production. 

                               Primary Sector 
This is where raw materials is extracted from nature. Activities include: 
- Agriculutre
- Fishing 
- Forestry 
- Mining
- Oil and gas extraction.  


                            Secondary Sector 
Tranfrom raw materisl into finished or semi-finished goods. Activities include: 
- Manufacturing
- Construction
- Food processing 
- Steel, chemicals
- Production of energy 

                               Tertiary Sector 
Provide services rather tha goods. Activities include: 
- Retail and Wholesale 
- Transport and Logistics 
- Healthcare
- Education
- Hospitality 
- Finance and Insurance 


                              Quaternary  Sector 
High level of knowledge based economy. Activities include: 
- IT 
- Data and Analytics 
- Research and Development 
- Consulting 
- Scientific services


                                Quinary Sector 
High level decision making and leadership roles. Activities include: 
- Government leadership 
- University leadership 
- Policy Think tanks 
- International Organisations
